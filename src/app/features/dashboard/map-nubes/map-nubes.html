What is the Cloud Map?

This map, typically obtained from satellite images, shows the distribution and coverage of clouds over a region. It uses different channels (visible, infrared) to show not only where the clouds are, but also characteristics like their thickness, altitude, and temperature. It is essential for identifying fronts, storms, and clear weather zones. ☁️

Positive Aspects

  • • Provides a broad and continuous view of meteorological systems.
  • • Allows for the identification of the formation and progression of fronts, cyclones, and hurricanes.
  • • The infrared channel helps detect clouds with great vertical development (storms) even at night.
  • • It is key for short and medium-term weather forecasting.

Negative Aspects

  • • The visible image is useless at night.
  • • It can be difficult to distinguish between low clouds, fog, and snow on the ground without expert analysis.
  • • It does not directly measure precipitation; it only shows the clouds that could generate it.
  • • The resolution may be insufficient to detect very small or localized phenomena.
